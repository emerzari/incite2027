\textbf{Executive Summary}: \\
\justify
Advanced nuclear energy is a national priority because of its potential to provide reliable, abundant energy while strengthening U.S. technological leadership and energy security. However, the design, safety assessment, and licensing of advanced reactors remain challenging due to the limited availability of experimental data and the complexity of the underlying thermal-fluid phenomena. High-fidelity simulation has therefore become an essential tool for accelerating reactor development and reducing uncertainty.

This project will leverage the capabilities of Frontier and Aurora to generate benchmark-quality datasets for key reactor thermal-hydraulic challenges, including thermal mixing, natural circulation, bypass flow, and transient heat transport. The largest simulations will contain more than one billion spectral elements, making them among the largest reactor-flow calculations ever performed.

Beyond advancing scientific understanding, the primary goal of the project is to develop and validate multiscale modeling methodologies. High-fidelity simulations provide unparalleled insight into reactor physics, but they remain too computationally expensive for routine engineering applications. The proposed work will therefore create the datasets needed to develop reduced-order models, data-driven closures, and other scale-bridging approaches that transfer information from high-fidelity simulations to practical reactor analysis tools.

The project builds on the DOE Nuclear Energy Advanced Modeling and Simulation (NEAMS) program and utilizes NekRS, a highly scalable GPU-based simulation code that has demonstrated excellent performance on both Frontier and Aurora. By combining validated experiments, exascale simulation, and multiscale modeling, the proposed work will help establish the next generation of predictive tools for advanced reactor design, safety analysis, and licensing.
